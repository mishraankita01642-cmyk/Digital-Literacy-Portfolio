### **Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud**

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details. It has become increasingly widespread with the rise of digital payments.

A typical UPI fraud happens in several steps. First, the fraudster contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or messaging app, often pretending to be a bank official, customer care executive, or even a buyer/seller. Next, they create urgency or offer an attractive deal (such as a refund, cashback, or prize). Then, they send a payment request or a malicious link and instruct the victim to approve it or enter their UPI PIN, claiming it is needed to “receive money.” In reality, entering the PIN authorizes a payment from the victim’s account. Once the victim completes this step, the money is instantly transferred to the fraudster’s account.

These scams usually target students, elderly people, and individuals who are new to digital payments or unaware of online security practices.

The consequences can be severe, including financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in digital systems. In many cases, recovering the lost money is difficult. This highlights the importance of awareness—users should never share their UPI PIN, click on unknown links, or approve unexpected payment requests.
